Popular Banarasi Foods

KACHORI SABZI

Banaras’s most iconic breakfast, served hot and spicy. You can try this anywhere but make sure you are having it hot only.

JALEBI

Sweet, crispy jalebis made fresh in desi ghee. Take this as a dessert of Kachori Sabji.

BANARASI PAAN

World-famous paan known for its fragrance & flavor. A One-time experience but a must try.

TAMATAR CHAAT

A spicy & tangy Varanasi special chaat made with tomatoes. Famous of Kashi Chaat Bhandaar at Godowliya.

ALOO TIKKI CHAAT

Drinking tea in Banaras is more than just a beverage; it's a cultural experience, often enjoyed from street vendors in narrow alleys.

CHHOTI KACHORI

Spicy and tasty as Kachori Sabji. You can find this in streets of Kal Bhairav Mandir.

CHAI(TEA)

Mostly known as Chowk ki chai and found at chowk. Drinking tea in Banaras is more than just a beverage; it's a cultural experience, often enjoyed from street vendors in narrow alleys.

BANARASI LASSI

Thick creamy lassi served in clay cups (kulhad). Famous of Pahalwan Lassi at Lanka.

COLD COFFEE

Very Famous as VT ki Coffee(VT stands for Vishwanath Temple). You can enjoy inside BHU outside Vishwanath Temple.

MALAIYO

light, airy, and sweet, making it a must-try delicacy in the cold season. Famous of Shree Ji. 

MAKKHAN TOAST

a famous street food breakfast in Banaras. You can enjoy it with your Tea at Chowk.

BAATI CHOKA

Baati Chokha is a highly renowned and authentic traditional food item. You can have it Baati Chokha Restaurant at Teliyabaag or its other branches too.
