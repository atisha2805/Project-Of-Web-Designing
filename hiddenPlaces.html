Hidden Places in Banaras

Explore the lesser-known yet beautiful corners of Banaras

Ramnagar Fort

Ramnagar Fort is a lesser-explored historical site located on the eastern bank of the Ganga River. Built in the 18th century, it reflects Mughal-style architecture and houses a museum displaying royal artifacts, vintage cars, and manuscripts. The fort offers a peaceful atmosphere away from crowded ghats.

Manikarnika Ghat

Manikarnika Ghat is one of the oldest and most sacred ghats in Varanasi.It is considered the main cremation ghat of the city.According to Hindu belief, cremation at Manikarnika Ghat gives moksha (liberation from the cycle of birth and death).

Ramnagar Bridge Viewpoint

The Ramnagar Bridge viewpoint provides a beautiful panoramic view of the Ganga River, especially during sunrise and sunset. It is a quiet spot preferred by locals and photographers to enjoy the scenic beauty of Varanasi.

Sankatha Ji Temple

Sankatha Ji Temple is a famous temple in Varanasi dedicated to Goddess Sankatha, a form of Goddess Durga. The temple is located near Manikarnika Ghat. Goddess Sankatha is believed to protect devotees from troubles and obstacles.
